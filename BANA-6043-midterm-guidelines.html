Midterm Guidelines

Matt Risley

  1. Midterm will be a paper-based examination with 20 questions.

    • 12 short answer and T/F questions on code chunks (R/SAS)
    • 3 questions that ask you to interpret output from R/SAS
    • 6 conceptual questions

  2. It is open notes (but not open computer).

  3. You can work individually or in a group with up to three people. I am not assigning groups, but if you want to work in a group and don’t know anyone, let me know ahead of time.

  4. Each student will have an individual test, but the test will have a randomly generated set of 20 questions. This means not everyone will have the same questions for their midterm. Your grade is based on the answers to your test, but you can use the other members in your group as a resource to complete it.

  5. There will be a strict time limit of 60 minutes for 20 questions. This means you individually have 3 minutes to answer each question. However, this also means that if you work in a group of 4 and each question is different (for 80 different questions), you would have 40 seconds per question if the group were to work on each question together. In other words, the key to success on the midterm is to work individually where possible and to work as a group only when you need to.

  6. Undergraduate students will receive a different test than graduate students. The test for students enrolled for undergraduate credit will be aligned with expectations for undergraduate courses versus graduate courses. Of course, these students can still work with others, including grad students, if they’d like.
