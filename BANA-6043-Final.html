Overview

You will work individually or with another student in the class to produce a concise analysis of a dataset using tools you learned in the course. You can use R, SAS, or a combination of both.

From the dataset,

  • Students enrolled in BANA6043 will select one continuous dependent variable and 2-3 independent variables.

  • Students enrolled in BANA5143 will select one continuous dependent variable and one independent variable.

Remember,

  • your independent variable is the variable you wish to explain

  • your dependent variable(s) will explain your independent variable

The objective of this project is to provide insights about your dependent variable through your dependent variables.

An insight does not mean you have to “find” something. In fact, you can start with a reasonable hypothesis, and if the data do not support it, then that is an insight.

The main components of the report are described below.

The deliverables will entail a report, your dataset, and your code.

Output without explanations will receive a very low grade! I’d be out of a job if I gave my boss a report that contained output with no explanations.

Explanations without output will receive a very low grade! I’d be out of a job if I gave my boss a report that contained explanation with no output.

Analysis (4 page maximum, inclusive of graphs/tables) 50%

This section is entirely bivariate and multivariate.

  1. examine your hypotheses with graphical output
    • you should provide a soft conclusion from this output as to whether your hypotheses are supported
    • ex: boxplots of your continuous dependent variable by a categorical independent variable (could also bin a continuous independent variable)
    • ex: scatterplots of your continuous dependent variable
  2. test your hypotheses
    • use t-tests, ANOVA, and/or regression
    • you should provide a soft conclusion from this output as to whether your hypotheses are supported

An analysis that includes the kitchen sink and poor/minimal explanations is inferior to a very thorough discussion and analysis of a single test.

The point is to tell the story, not to show me you can run all tests available to you.


Conclusion (one paragraph maximum) - 10%

You should provide a firm conclusion from your analysis as to whether your hypotheses are supported.

Your possible outcomes are:

  1. your analysis definitively supports your hypotheses (a relationship exists)

  2. your analysis definitively does not support your hypotheses (a relationship does not exist)

  3. your analysis weakly supports your hypotheses (a relationship may exist)

You must say how and why you arrived at your outcome!!
